IBM corporate history

IBM (International Business Machines Corporation) was founded in 1911 under the name Computing-Tabulating-Recording Company (CTR) which was formed from the merger of several companies engaged in the fields of calculating, recording and data processing. In 1924, the company changed its name to IBM and focused on developing computer technology. IBM was the world's largest information technology company in the 1970s and 1980s, creating innovative products such as mainframes, personal computers, and operating systems. In the 1990s, IBM went into decline and underwent major restructuring to strengthen its position. Currently, IBM focuses on information technology services and provides business solutions, cloud computing, and cyber security.

Intel Corporate History

The Intel Corporation was founded in 1968 by Robert Noyce and Gordon Moore, who at the time were leading engineers at the semiconductor company Fairchild Semiconductor. Intel focused on the development and production of microprocessors, which were originally used for personal computers. In 1981, Intel released the 8086 microprocessor used in the IBM PC computer, which became the industry standard at the time. In the following decades, Intel continued to develop microprocessor technology and launch innovative products such as Pentium and Core. Apart from that, Intel also invests in other technologies such as flash memory and artificial intelligence. Today, Intel remains a major player in the semiconductor industry, and continues to innovate to meet increasingly complex technology needs.

Google Corporate History

Google was founded in 1998 by Larry Page and Sergey Brin, who were doctoral students at Stanford University. They created an innovative and effective search engine, using a PageRank algorithm that takes into account the interrelationships between web pages. Google quickly grew into a successful company and continues to grow by launching various products and services such as Google Maps, Google AdWords, Google Chrome, and Android. In 2004, Google made an initial public offering (IPO) and became a public company. Currently, Google is part of Alphabet Inc. and remains one of the world's largest technology companies, with a focus on developing search technology, online advertising, artificial intelligence, and cloud computing services.

Microsoft Corporate History

Microsoft Corporation was founded in 1975 by Bill Gates and Paul Allen. Initially, Microsoft produced software for personal computers, and in 1981, launched the MS-DOS operating system which became standard at the time. In 1985, Microsoft launched the Windows operating system which became one of the largest and most successful products in the history of computer technology. Microsoft also produces productivity software such as Microsoft Office and Internet Explorer. In the 2000s, Microsoft entered the game console market with the launch of the Xbox and focused on cloud computing services with Azure. Today, Microsoft remains one of the world's largest technology companies and develops products and services such as Windows 10, Surface, and Microsoft Teams.

Computer History

The history of computers begins in the 19th century, when Charles Babbage developed an automatic calculating machine called the Analytical Engine. However, the achine was never finished. At the beginning of the 20th century, the IBM company produced the first vacuum tube computer in 1951 which was used for military purposes In the 1960s, the first transistor computers were launched and replaced the vacuum tubes, which made computers smaller and more efficient. In the 1970s, the microprocessor was invented and allowed the development of smaller and less expensive personal computers. Then in the 1980s and 1990s, personal computers grew in popularity and became affordable for the general public. In the late 20th and early 21st centuries, technological developments accelerated with the emergence of the internet and mobile technology, resulting in computer devices such as laptops, tablets and smartphones that were very popular and became an essential part of modern life.

The dark side of the Internet

The dark side of the internet is that part of the internet that is not accessible through search engines like Google and can only be accessed using certain software, such as Tor. On the dark side of the internet, many illegal activities such as drug trafficking, weapons and personal information are stolen and sold. In addition, the dark side of the internet is also a gathering place for extremist and terrorist groups who use the internet as a tool to spread their ideology. This has led to worldwide concern about how to deal with the threats that arise from the dark side of the internet and how to deal with the crimes that are committed within it. Nonetheless, the dark side of the internet also has positive uses, such as enabling users to maintain their privacy and security in using the internet and becoming an important tool for journalists and human rights activists to expose crimes and human rights violations that occur around the world.

Initially founded by Android

Android was first developed by Andy Rubin, Rich Miner, Nick Sears, and Chris White in 2003 in Palo Alto, California. Initially, they planned to develop a digital camera operating system, but then changed their focus to developing an operating system for mobile devices. In 2005, Google bought Android Inc., the company Rubin and friends formed to develop Android, and acquired the Android development team. In 2007, Android was officially released and became the main competitor of Apple's iOS. Android is currently the most popular mobile operating system in the world and is used in various types of mobile devices such as smartphones, tablets and smartwatches.

What is IOT

IoT stands for "Internet of Things". It is a term used to describe the network of physical devices, vehicles, home appliances, and other items that are embedded with sensors, software, and connectivity to allow for the exchange of data over the internet. The main goal of IoT is to create a seamless connection between the physical and digital worlds, enabling devices to communicate with each other and with humans in real-time. This has led to the development of new products and services that can improve efficiency, productivity, and convenience in various industries, such as healthcare, transportation, manufacturing, and home automation.

History of Asus Company

ASUS is a multinational computer hardware and electronics company based in Taiwan. It was founded in 1989 by four computer engineers, Ted Hsu, M.T. Liao, Wayne Hsieh, and T.H. Tung. The name "ASUS" is derived from the word "Pegasus," the winged horse in Greek mythology. In the early years, ASUS primarily produced computer motherboards, and it quickly became one of the top motherboard manufacturers in the world. In 1997, ASUS launched its first laptop computer, the ASUS P6300, which was one of the first laptops to feature a Pentium processor.
